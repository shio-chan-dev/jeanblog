# Comparison Axes

Choose 3-5 axes that matter to the actual judgment. Do not pad the article with
every possible dimension.

## High-Signal Axes

- Problem addressed
  - What pain exists before the new tool or workflow is introduced?
- Retrieval value
  - What context becomes easier to recover?
- Source-of-truth fitness
  - Can this thing hold authoritative knowledge, or is it only an auxiliary layer?
- Traceability
  - Does it improve or weaken issue/doc/commit traceability?
- Maintenance cost
  - What new operational burden does it introduce?
- Team collaboration fitness
  - Is it suitable for an individual only, or safe as a team default?
- Adoption cost
  - How hard is it to trial, integrate, or keep running?
- Failure mode
  - What goes wrong when people over-trust it?

## Organization Rule

When comparing a candidate against an incumbent workflow, prefer this pattern:

1. State the axis.
2. Explain the incumbent behavior.
3. Explain the candidate behavior.
4. State the judgment for that axis.

Avoid long "system A" and "system B" blocks that force the reader to do the
comparison work alone.
